Cricket is more than leather and willow,
More than a scoreboard’s flickering glow.
It lives in the roar of a crowded stand,
In dust-stained whites and a trembling hand.

The bowler runs with thunderous pace,
Eyes fixed firm on the batter’s face.
The seam cuts sharp through evening light,
A battle born in skill and fight.

The batter waits with silent grace,
Heartbeats echo in that small space.
One perfect stroke, the crowd takes flight,
The ball sails singing into the night.

Children dream on narrow streets,
With broken bats and restless feet.
Every alley becomes a ground,
Where future legends may be found.

Through victories grand and losses grim,
The spirit of cricket never grows dim.
For every match, in joy or pain,
Teaches hearts to rise again.
